\subsection{Group Schemes}

\indent The primary objects of interest for us when it comes to N\'eron models will be group schemes.
In this section we introduce group schemes from the functor of points view, and also show that its equivalence to the traditional definitions involving maps between products.
As an use, this will give us natural ways of defining multiplication by $n$ and translation maps for arbitrary group schemes.\newline
\indent Towards the end of this section, we introduce the notion of arithmetic surfaces.
A major theorem will be the existence of certain minimal such ones for nice enough curves, which we will later use to show the existence of N\'eron models for elliptic curves over a fraction field of a Dedekind domain.

\begin{definition}
	An $S$-scheme $G$ is a group scheme if the functor 
	\[\text{Hom}(-, G): \textbf{Sch/S}^{\textbf{op}} \to \textbf{Set}\]
	factors through the forgetful functor from $\textbf{Group}$ to $\textbf{Set}$.
\end{definition}

In other words, for any $S$-scheme $T$, the set of $T$-points of $G$ forms a group, which is functorial in $T$.
We will show its equivalence to the usual definitions in the more general setting of a category with finite products.

\subsection{Arithmetic Surfaces}

Given a scheme $X$ over Spec($K$), our interest will be in "lifts" of $X$ to a scheme $X'$ over a scheme $S$ with function field $K$ such that its generic fiber $X'_K$ is isomorphic to $X$, which we will call \textit{$S$-models} of $X$.  
We define one nice example of such a lift through Arithmetic surfaces; N\'eron models will also be such objects as we will later encounter.



\begin{definition}
	Let $C/K$ be a non-singular connected projective curve. 
	An \textit{arithmetic surface} (over $R$) is an integral, normal, excellent scheme that is flat and finite type over $\text{Spec}(R)$ whose generic fiber is isomorphic to $C/K$ and whose special fibers are unions of curves.
\end{definition}

\section{N\'eron Models}

Throughout this section, we will work as generally as we can, letting $S$ denote a \textit{Dedekind domain} (noetherian normal scheme of dimension $\leq 1$) as in \cite{BLR}, unless otherwise specified. 
Note then all local rings of $S$ are either fields or discrete valuation rings, in particular $S$ is always regular.
We denote $K = \prod_{i} K(\eta_i)$ be the \textit{ring of rational fractions} for $\eta_i$ the finitely many generic points of the irreducible components of $S$. 
For any scheme $Y$ over $S$, we will denote $Y_K$ for its generic fiber $Y \times_{S} \text{Spec}(K)$. \newline

\indent As mentioned in our discussion on arithmetic surfaces, for a scheme $X$ over Spec($K$), our interest will be in models of schemes $X$ over $\text{Spec}(K)$ which can be though of as lifts $X'$ over $S$ with generic fiber $X'_K$ is isomorphic to $X$. 
In the case of the question of whether there is a notion of a minimal such model, one potential answer was given in \Cref{min-as}. 
Another notion comes in the form of N\'eron Models.

\begin{definition}[N\'eron Model]\label{def:NM}
	Let $X_K$ be a smooth and separated $K$-scheme of finite type. A \textit{N\'eron model} of $X_K$ is an $S$-model of $X$  of $X_K$ which is smooth, separated, and of finite type which satisfies the following universal property (which we call the N\'eron mapping property).\\
	\indent \textbf{For each smooth $S$-scheme $Y$ and $K$-morphism $u_K: Y_K \to X_K$, there exists a unique $S$-morphism $u: Y \to X$ extending $u_K$}.
\end{definition}

\noindent Immediately from its definition we have the following important facts.

\begin{proposition}
Let $X$ be a N\'eron model for its generic fiber $X_K$. Then the following hold:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $X$ is uniquely determined by $X_K$ up to unique isomorphism.
  \item The natural map $X(S) \to X_K(K)$ is bijective, in other words every $K$-point of $X_K$ lifts to an unique $S$-point of $X$. 
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	The proof of (1) is the usual one for objects defined by universal properties. Namely, for two such models $X$ and $X'$ the identity morphisms  on their generic fibers induce maps $f:X \to X'$ and $g: X' \to X$ from the N’eron mapping property. Then $f \circ g$ and $g \circ f$ are unique lifts of the identity morphisms of the generic fibers, but the identity morphism already satisfies this so they must be equal. 
	Hence $f \circ g = \text{id}_{X'}$ and $g \circ f = \text{id}_{X}$ which shows $X$ and $X'$ are isomorphic. \newline
	\indent To prove (2), note that $S$ is smooth over itself, now applying the N\'eron mapping property for its generic fiber $\text{Spec}(K)$ tells us precisely every $K$-point of $X_K$ lifts uniquely to an $S$-point of $X$.
\end{proof}

When verifying that that a scheme satisifes the N\'eron mapping property, we show that we in fact do not need to worry about uniqueness of the lifts; this relies on the separatedness of N\'eron models and the condition that we only worry about maps from smooth schemes. 

\begin{proposition}\label{unique-extension}
Let $X_K$ be a smooth and separated $K$-scheme of finite type and $X$ an $S$-model of $X_K$ which is smooth, separated, and of finite type.
Suppose that for any smooth $S$-scheme $Y$ and $K$-morphism $u_K: Y_K \to X_K$ that there exists some $S$-morphism $u: Y \to X$ extending $u_K$, then $u_K$ is unique, hence $X$ is a N\'eron model for $X_K$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	Consider any two possible extensions $u_1, u_2: Y \to X$ extending $u_K$.
	We first show that the generic fiber $Y_K \subset Y$ is dense in $Y$.
	Indeed, since $Y$ is flat and locally of finite presentation over $S$ (as it is smooth over $S$) its structure morphism is an open morphism [\cite{SP}, Lemma 29.25.10]. 
Then every nonempty open $U$ of $Y$ maps to a nonempty open in $S$, which must contain a generic point, i.e. $U$ intersects the generic fiber hence it is dense. \newline
\indent Now note that $S$ is regular being a Dedekind scheme, hence $Y$ is regular as it is smooth over $S$: in particular it is reduced. 
Now the Reduced-To-Separated Theorem [\cite{FOAG}, 11.3.2] shows any two maps which extends $u$ must be the same.
To be more precise, one can show that the closed subscheme $E$ which $u_1$ and $u_2$ agree on is the limit of the below diagram 
\[\begin{tikzcd}
	E & X \\
	Y & {X \times_S X}
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=1-2]
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=2-1]
	\arrow["\lrcorner"{anchor=center, pos=0.125}, draw=none, from=1-1, to=2-2]
	\arrow["\delta", from=1-2, to=2-2]
	\arrow["{u_1 \times u_2}"', from=2-1, to=2-2]
\end{tikzcd}\]
which upon base-changing to $K$ (which preserves fiber products), shows that the generic fiber factors through $E$, hence $E$ is a closed subscheme containing all of $Y$, and must be all of $Y$ as it is reduced.
\end{proof}

Thus to verify the N\'eron mapping property, we only need to show existence of lifts which we will make us of later on.

\begin{proposition}\label{weil-extension}
	Let $S$ be a normal noetherian base scheme, and $u: Z \to G$ an $S$-rational map from a smooth $S$-scheme $Z$ to a smooth and separated $S$-group scheme $G$.
Then the set of points in $Z$ where $u$ is not defined if pure codimension 1.
In particular, if $u$ is defined in codimension $\leq 1$, it is defined everywhere.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	This proof is a bit long and involved, so we we will only give an overview of the argument; for the complete proof see [\cite{BLR}, 4.4, Theorem 1].
	We first assume the proposition in the case when $G$ is any affine scheme, this is proved by using the fact that $Z$ is also locally noetherian being locally of finite type over $S$ and normal as it is regular.
	And as a result any rational map from $Z$ to an affine scheme defined codimension $\leq 1$ is defined everywhere using the fact that rational functions defined over codimension $\leq 1$ are defined everywhere for locally noetherian and normal schemes [see \cite{FOAG}, 13.5.I]. \newline
	\indent Now in the general case, we can use the fact that $G$ is a group scheme to exploit the affine case.
	Assume that $u$ is defined in codimension $\geq 2$.
	Let $U$ denote the maximal domain of definition of $U$.
	Consider the rational map $v: Z \times_S Z \to G$ which is the composition $u \times (u \circ i)\,:\,Z \times_S  Z \to G \times G$ composed with the the multiplication morphism $\mu: G \times G \to G$.
	Let $F$ denote the maximal domain of definition of $V$, then clearly $U \times U$ lies in it.
	The main thing that we show  is that the image of the diagonal map $\Delta: Z \to Z \times_S Z$ lies in $F$.
	Denote $A$ the image of the diagonal $\Delta$ which we will sometimes identify with $Z$; we want to show $F \ic A = \emptyset$
	Now the map $Z \xrightarrow{\Delta} Z \times_S Z \xrightarrow{v} G$ from construction factors through the unit section $F: S \to G$. 
	Assume for sake of contradiciton there is a $x \in F \ic A$ and let $s$ denotes its image in $S$.
	We may choose an affine open $H$ containing $e(s) \in G$, whose pre-image in $Z \times_S Z$ we denote as $W$.
	Then our rational map $v$ restricted to $W$ factors through the affine $H$, so applying our initial assumption, the locus $F'$ where it isn't defined is of pure codimension 1.
	Considering $F' \ic A$, it must contain $U \subset Z$ (once again identifying $A$ with $Z$) which is of codimension $\geq 2$ in $A$ by assumption.
	However, as $F'$ is of pure codimension 1, one can show its intersection with $A$ also drops the codimension at most 1, a contradiction.
	Thus $F$ contains diagonal $A$ as claimed. \newline
\indent Now all that remains is to show that that $U = Z$.
Using [\cite{BLR}, 2.5, Proposition 5] it suffices to give a faithfully flat $S$-morphism $f:Z' \to Z$ where $Z'$ is an $S$-scheme smooth of finite type such that $u \circ f$ is defined everywhere.
Our choice is $Z'$ is the intersection of $V$ with $Z \times_S U \subset Z \times_S Z$, with the map being the projection along the first factor.
This factor as an open immersion into a quotient map $Z \times_S Z \to Z$ which is smooth, hence their composite is flat.
Thus to show faithful flatness all that remains is surjectivity which can be done by considering a geometric point of $Z$, and noting $V$ contains the diagonal and $U$ is open in $Z$.
This map coincides with $Z' \to G$, written in coordinates as $(z_1, z_2) \to v(z_1, z_2)u(z_2)$, and is is defined everywhere as claimed.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:weil-extension}
	Suppose that $G$ as in \Cref{weil-extension} is also proper. 
	Then any $S$-rational map $u: Z \to G$ is defined everywhere.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
	From \Cref{weil-extension}, it suffices to show that $u$ is defined in codimension $\geq 2$.
	Equivalently, $u$ is defined at the generic point $\eta$ of any irreducible subset $I \subset X$ of codimension 1.
	Note that since $Z$ is regular its irreducible components are disjoint; since it is normal and locally noetherian as well being smooth over $S$ (which is Noetherian), each irreducible component (also the connected components) are open. 
	Thus we may consider the case when $Z$ is irreducible, as if we show the rational map extends along each irreducible component it extends everywhere.\newline
\indent
	Consider the stalk $\mc{O}_{Z, \eta}$, by regularity of $Z$ is a discrete valuation ring and denote the image of the generic point of $Z$ in $G$ as $y$.
	The map to $G$ factors through scheme-theoretic image of $u$, as $Z$ is reduced, the underlying set of the scheme-theoretic closure is equal to the closure of $y$ [\cite{FOAG}, 9.4.5], so it then suffices to assume $u$ is a dominant rational map.	
	Note that $G$ is still proper being a closed subscheme of a proper $S$-scheme.
	This induces maps of functions fields $\text{Spec}(K(Z)) \to \text{Spec}K((G))$, and applying the valuative criterion of properness to the diagram
\[\begin{tikzcd}
	{\text{Spec}(\mc{O}_{Z, \eta})} & G \\
	{\text{Spec}((K(Z))} & {\text{Spec}(K(G))}
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=1-2]
	\arrow[from=1-1, to=2-1]
	\arrow[from=1-2, to=2-2]
	\arrow[from=2-1, to=2-2]
\end{tikzcd}\]
the rational map extends to a rational map $\mc{O}_{Z, \eta} \to Y$.
We can spread this out to a rational map $Z \to Y$ which agrees on maps of function fields with $u$, hence are equivalent rational maps.
Thus $Z$ is defined in codimension 1 and so extends everywhere.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}\label{etale-basechange}
	N\'eron models commute with \'etale base change, i.e. if $\alpha: S' \to S’$ is  \'etale, with $K'$ the ring of rational functions on $S'$, then $X_{S'} = X \times_S S'$ is a N\'eron model for $X_{K'} = X_K \times_{\text{Spec(K)}} \text{Spec}(K')$. 
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	We wish to show that $X_{S'}$ satisfies the N\'eron mapping property, so let $Y'$ denote a smooth $S'$ scheme and consider a $K'$ morphism $f': Y'_{K'} \to X_{K'}$ which we want to show uniquely lifts. 
	Note that $Y'$ can be naturally viewed a scheme over $S$ via the map $\alpha: S \to S'$, as $\alpha$ is \'etale (hence smooth) then $Y'$ is also smooth over $S$ (as smooth morphisms are preserved by composition).
	Now by the N\'eron mapping property for $X$, the $K$-map $Y'_{K'} \to X_{K'} \to X_K$, (the latter morphism the usual projection) lifts to an unique $S$-morphism $Y' \to X$ extending it.
	Combined with the structure morphism $Y' \to S'$ (which can be viewed as an $S$-morphism) we get a map $f: Y' \to X \times_{S} S' = X_{S'}$ which one can straightforwardly verify extends $f'$.
	By \Cref{unique-extension}, we have that $X_{S'}$ is a N\'eron model for $X_{K'}$ as claimed.
	\newline
\end{proof}

As an example, as open immersions are \'etale, for any open subscheme $S' \subset S$ we have that $X \otimes_S S'$ is a N\'eron model over $S'$. 

We will need to make use of the following lemma.
\begin{lemma}[EGA IV, 8.8.2]\label{EGA-lemma}
	Let $s$ be a point of a scheme $S$. Then
\begin{enumerate}
	\item If $X,Y$ are $S$-schemes of finite presentation, then the canonical map
		\[\varinjlim \text{Hom}_{S'}(X \times_S S'. Y \times_S S') \to \text{Hom}_{\mc{O}_{S,s}}(X \otimes_S \mc{O}_{S,s}, Y \otimes_S \mc{O}_{S,s}) \]
	taken over all open neighborhoods $S'$ of $s$ in $S$ is bijective.
\item Let $X'$ be an $\mc{O}_{S,s}$-scheme of finite presentation.
	Then there is an open neigborhood $S'$ of $s$ in $S$ and an $S'$-scheme $X'$ of finite presentation such that $X \otimes_{S'} \mc{O}_{S,s}$ is isomorphic to $X'$.
\end{enumerate}

\end{lemma}

We will not give a proof of the lemma here, but we note it uses finite presentation (finitely many generators and relations) to allows use of the method of "spreading out" from the stalk $\mc{O}_{S,s}$ to a Zariski open containing it. 
We will use it in the proof of propositions such as the following.

\begin{proposition}\label{lg:closed}
	Let $S$ be a Dedekind Scheme and $X$ an $S$-scheme of finite type.
	Then the following are equivalent
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item $X$ is a N\'eron model of its generic fiber
		\item For each closed point $s \in S$, the $\mc{O}_{S,s}$-scheme $X \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s}$ is a N\'eron model of its generic fiber.
	\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	We first show that 1. implies 2. and so select any closed point $s \in S$.
	To show that $X \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s}$ is a N\'eron model for its generic fiber (in fact the same as the one for $X$), consider any smooth $\mc{O}_{S,s}$ scheme $Y_s$ and $K$-morphism $u_K$ from its generic fiber $Y_{s,K}$ to $X_K$.
	We just want to construct a lift of $u_K$, so we may assume that $Y$ is quasicompact (so finitely presetned over $\mc{O}_{S,s}$), as we may cover $Y$ by such sets (even affine opens), and a lift of $U_K$ along this open cover glues together.
	Now by \Cref{EGA-lemma}, (2), we can lift $Y_s$ to a scheme $Y_{S'}$ over an open $S' \subset S$ which we can take to be connected, whose fiber over $\mc{O}_{S,s}$ is $Y_s$.
	As $Y_s$ is smooth over $\mc{O}_{S,s}$, so locally the map is of the form $S'[y_1, \dots, y_m]/(g_1, \dots, g_s) \to \mc{O}_{S,s}[x_1, \dots, x_{n+r}]/(f_1, \dots, f_r)$ where the determinant of the Jacobian matrix of the $f_i$ in $x_1, \dots, x_r$ is invertible. 
	We can spread to some smaller open of $S'$ where it is smooth (by inverting the finitely many denominators appearing in coefficients of the $f_i, g_i$ and images of $y_i$) so we may assume further that $Y_{S'}$ is smooth over $S'$.
	By \Cref{etale-basechange} $Y_{S'}$ is a N\'eron model for its generic fiber, which is the same as $Y_{s'}$, thus $u_K$ lifts up to a map $u': Y_{S'} \to X \times_S S'$, in which case the map $u' \times \mc{O}_{S,s}: Y_{S'} \times \mc{O}_{S,s} \to X \times \mc{O}_{S.s}$ is a map from $Y_{s'}$ extending $u_K$, so it is a N\'eron model by \Cref{unique-extension}.\newline
	\indent Conversely, assume that $X \times_X \mc{O}_{S,s}$ is a N\'eron model of its generic fiber for all $s$. 
	Consider any smooth $S$-scheme $Y$ and a map $u_K: Y_K \to X_K$ of the generic fibers. These (perhaps restricting to generic points) are maps of generic fibers of $Y \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s} \to X \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s}$, so lift to unique extension of $u_K$ for each $s$. By \Cref{EGA-lemma},(a) these lift to maps $Y \times_S S' \to X \times_S S'$ for some open $S'$ containing $s$. Once again, they can be glued together to give a map $Y \to X$ extending $u_K$ and so $X$ is a N\'eron model for its generic model by \Cref{unique-extension}.
\end{proof}

Thus the existence of a global N\'eron model $X$ gives us the existence of all local N\'eron models $X \times \mc{O}_{S,s}$ and furthermore being a N\'eron model for its generic fiber can be checked locally.

\begin{proposition}
	Let $S$ be a Dedekind scheme with ring of rational functions $K$, and let $X_K$ be a smooth and separated $K$-scheme of finite type.
	Then the following are equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}
	\item There exists a global N\'eron model $X$ of $X_K$ over $S$.
	\item There exists a dense open subscheme $S' \subset S$ such that $X_K$ admits a N\'eron model over $S'$ as well as N\'eron models for the (finitely many) closed points of $S - S'$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	(1) implies (2) using \Cref{etale-basechange} and the fact that open immersions are \'etale and \Cref{lg:closed}.
	To prove the other direction, consider a smooth $S$-scheme $Y$ and $u_K: Y_K \to X_K$ a $K$-morphism of their generic fibers. 
	Now $u_K$ (up to perhaps restriction to generic point) are the maps of generic points on the $X_{S'} \colonequals X \times_S S'$ and $X \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s}$ so by the N\'eron mapping property have unique extensions of $u_K$.
	Consider any $s \in S - S'$, by \Cref{EGA-lemma},(a), the lift $Y \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s} \to X \times_S \mc{O}_{S,s}$ lifts to a map $Y \times_S U \to X \times_S U$ for an open $U \supset s$.
	Now these maps can be glued together on opens in $Y$, and so define a global lift of $u_K$ from $Y \to X$, hence $X$ is N\'eron model for its generic fiber as claimed.
\end{proof}


\section{Elliptic Curves}

In this section, we prove the existence of N\'eron models for elliptic curves over $K$, the fraction field of a Dedekind domain $R$. 
The majority of our time will be spent in showing the existence for the local case where $R$ is a discrete valuation rings, which will requires the introduction of the notion of special henselization. \newline

Before we get too deep, let's just consider the problem of constructing $S$-models of $K$-schemes without worrying about them having nice properties quite yet.
For simplicity, assume that $S = \text{Spec}(R)$ for $R$ a Dedekind domain.
In the affine case, suppose we have $X \cong \text{Spec}(K[x_1, \dots, x_n]/I)$, where we may let $I = (f_1, \dots, f_n)$ have finitely many generators by noetherianity. 
We do the most natural thing and note that we can scale the finitely many $f_i$ by a nonzero common multiple (though not unique) $r \in R$ such that each $rf_i \in R[x]$.
In this way, we have that $R[x]/(rf_1, \dots, rf_n)$ is a model for $X$ as the generic fiber is the spectrum of 
\[K \otimes_K R[x]/(rf_1, \dots, rf_n) = K[x]/(rf_1, \dots, rf_n) = K[x]/(f_1, \dots, f_n)\]
as $r$ is a unit in $K$. \newline

\indent Similarly, in the projective case when $K \cong \text{Proj}(K[x_1, \dots, x_n]/(g_1, \dots, g_n))$ for homogeneous $g_i$ we can similarly scale them by nonzero (and still non-unique) $r \in R$ to obtain integral models $\text{Proj}(R[x_1, \dots, x_n]/(rg_1, \dots, rg_n))$  for $X$.
In fact, note that $X$ is still projective, so satisfies the property that $K$-points extend uniquely to $R$-points, an important consequence of the N\'eron mapping property ().



An elliptic curve $E/K$ is a projective curve, so as mentioned in the discussion above there are projective $\text{Spec}(R)$-models for $E$ which are potential candidates for N\'eron models.
In this section we will show that in the case when $E$ has good reduction the N\'eron mdoel can be recovered this way.
However, more work will need to be done when there is bad reduction.

\begin{definition}[(Strictly) Henselian Local Rings]
	A discrete valuation ring $R$ is called \textit{Henselian} if it satisfies Hensel's lemma.
	That is, for any monic polynomial $f(x) \in R[x]$ and $a \in R$ satisfying
	\[f(a) \equiv a \pmod{\mk{p}} \quad \quad f'(a) \not\equiv 0 \pmod{\mk{p}}\]
	there exists a unique element lift of $a$ to  a $\alpha \in R$ such that $f(\alpha) = 0$.
	Furthermore, if $R$ has separably closed residue field it is called \textit{strictly Henselian}.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}
	Let $(R, \mk{m}, k)$ be a discrete valuation ring. 
	Let $X/R$ be a smooth $R$-scheme and $\overline{X}/R$ it special fiber. 
	If $R$ is Henselian, then the reduction map
	\[X(R) \to \overline{X}(k)\]
	is surjective.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
	\cite{AEC2}, pg 342.
\end{proof}


\begin{proposition}[(Strict) Henselization of DVRs]

\end{proposition}
